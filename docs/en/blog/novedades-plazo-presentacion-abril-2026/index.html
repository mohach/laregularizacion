Application Period Open: How to Apply for Extraordinary Regularization Step by Step

The application period for extraordinary regularization remains open until June 30. We explain how to submit your application without mistakes.

noticias 🔥 Trending plazo presentación regularización 2026

The extraordinary regularization 2026 process is progressing normally and the application period remains open. Thousands of people have already started their procedures, but there is still enough time for those who haven't yet done so. The deadline is June 30, 2026, and it is advisable not to wait until the last weeks, when registry offices may become saturated and complicate the process.

Where to submit your application

You can submit your application in two ways:

  • In person at General State Administration registries, at regional offices with agreements in place, or at post offices. Some regions have set up specific appointment systems for this process.

  • Online through the electronic office, if you have a digital certificate or Cl@ve system.

In both cases, it is essential to bring all required documentation in order. An incomplete application may be rejected or significantly delay your case.

EX-31 and EX-32 forms: key differences

Although both forms are part of the same process, there are important differences:

EX-31 (Rooting/Arraigo): For those who can prove continuous stay in Spain and meet social, labor or training integration requirements. This is the most common form.

EX-32 (International agreements): For citizens of Ibero-American countries, Andorra, Philippines, Equatorial Guinea or Portugal who can benefit from dual nationality agreements or specific treaties.

Fee 790 Code 052

The fee payment is mandatory and must be made before submitting your application. It is €38.28 paid using form 790, code 052. You can pay at any collaborating bank. Keep the receipt, as it must be attached to your application.

Immediate effect upon registration

An important detail many overlook: from the moment you submit your complete application and receive the registration receipt, you are considered to have provisional residence and work authorization while your file is being processed. This means you can start working legally from that moment.
